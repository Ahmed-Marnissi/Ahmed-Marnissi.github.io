Theory
======

When two periodic signals are phase shifted, one appears as a time-shifted
version of the other. Cross-correlation estimates this time shift by measuring
similarity across lags. The peak correlation indicates the delay.

Cross-correlation
-----------------

.. math::

   R_{xy}(\tau) = \sum x(t)\,y(t+\tau)

Where:

- :math:`R_{xy}(\tau)` is the cross-correlation.
- :math:`x(t)` and :math:`y(t)` are the two signals.
- :math:`\tau` is the time shift.

Phase shift
-----------

Once the time delay :math:`t_d` is obtained from the peak index, the phase
shift in degrees is:

.. math::

   \theta = t_d \cdot f \cdot 360.0

Where :math:`f` is the signal frequency in Hz.
